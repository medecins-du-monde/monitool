Why an online platform
  • Structure the project's monitoring system
  • Increase reliability
    • Have consistency checks on data to limit entry errors
    • Limit mistakes in calculation
  • Step back
    • Encourage geographically remote projects to use the same indicators when measuring comparable events.
    • Have mechanisms to compare the value of an indicator on a project, with the same value for all other that collected it..
  • Ease communication
    • Provide direct access to project's data to all departments in the NGO (management, communication, ...)
    • Have single source of truth between headquarters and field project
  • Standardize
    • Ease turn-over by limiting the number of different tools used across projects.
    • Reduce the learning curve required to consult data on a new project.
Keep in mind

Monitool is all about monitoring.

It contributes to the ongoing, continuous and systematic monitoring of the situation and its evolution throughout the life of a project.

Its goal is to help taking decisions, thanks to the continuous follow-up of indicators that provide information on the progress of activities, and their contribution to the expected outputs and outcomes decided for your project.

Even if it can contribute to it, it's primary goal is not to do epidemiologic monitoring, or centralize morbidity sheets which are time-consuming and have little value in project management, specially for projects that support health structure (in opposition with providing direct care).

Getting started

Before setting your project up in Monitool, some preliminary steps can help you

  • Design your reference project: logical framework with output and outcomes indicators, process indicators can be added if necessary.
  • Use the Indicators summary tool to document all indicators from your reference project, and possibly donor's projects (if they are different).
    • Why: How is each indicator going to help measuring the output or outcome that is expected from your activities? How do you plan on analysing it (graphs, tables, with which periodicity: you need to plan in advance how your data will be used for decision making).
    • How: identifie all data and disagregations that will be needed to compute each one of your indicators.
    • When, where, by and for whom?
  • Build a dataflow chart from the indicator's summary to draw how data will flow in your project
    • The data sources organized according to the project's structure
    • Detail collected data and precise disagregations
    • Organize your indicators by logical framework
When should you use Monitool

Monitool plays a role during four stages of a project

  • Design of the logical frame
    Monitool provides all others projets logframes, with the list of used indicators and targets for each one of them.
  • Mise en place des outils de suivi
    Explicitely write down the path followed by your data and attach deadlines on data collection.
  • Suivi régulier
    Reporting graphs and tables of your choice are available and built with the most up-to-date data.
  • Post-fermeture
    Your project will be archived and available for future references.
